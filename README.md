# Final Commentary
## Why I chose LibreOffice
The biggest factor in my choosing LibreOffice as the tool for my final project was simple: it was super easy for me to learn the software's mechanics, layout, and formatting. It was a skill I picked up quickly because it was a mix between the standard Google Docs and the Microsoft Word Software, two tools which I am well-versed in as a technical writer major, as being adept in all genres and versions of popular writing software is a likely skill I will need in my future career.

Regarding my future career, I chose LibreOffice because it felt most relevant to the specific subplan in the TWC major. As an aspiring Environmental Scientist Technical Writer, I often felt tasked with creating book interiors, white papers, and reports that were almost identical to my final project, giving me projects to add to my portfolio for interviews and job hunting. My ability to organize and make complex topics, research, or ideas into a well-formatted and cohesive layout that could allow almost anyone to understand, follow along, and digest easily is why I wanted to get the most practice and time with LibreOffice, and this final project allowed me to go back and refresh my memory on these newer skills, so I don't forget them.

## Trade-offs, Decisions, Pros & Cons
One of the biggest trade-offs, a conscious decision, was changing the spacing, header font size, and style from the original document. This was because, as it was originally a group project, we all made compromises on how the document should look, flow, and be spaced out. I went back and reordered some of the Heading 1 titles because, as a technical and science writer, the white paper's order didn't sit right with me. I also removed the empty space between each header. Making the overall paper shorter, neater, and symetricall through out.

I think the only other trade-off I had to make was the font color of the heading levels 2 and 3. This was because, now that there were no gaps between the headers, as in the original, I didn't think the clash of back-to-back headers in different colors looked professional. I updated the paragraph and character styles so they are distinguishable from the body text, without being too flashy or disrupting the natural flow of the created headers.

However, the biggest positive this software has, in addition to everything already mentioned, and something I can't gush about, is its step-down mechanic. It is, in my opinion, the most useful tool in the entire software. It always makes me keep writing and not worry about the style of text I'm using, because it lets me set my own order of cascading starting paragraphs. It lets me set it all beforehand and then I can simple start typing and every time I hit enter or start a new line each custom edit I made will active, allowing me to never have to lift my hands from the keyboard, making my typing faster, precise, and efficient is a tool I wish Google Docs and Mircosoft Word had as intreqite of a tool as LibreOffice did. I also like to finish by noting that it is completely free, which Microsoft Word doesn't do, which is great news for me because I prefer to do my work and writing on that software, and this free alternative is just amazing!
